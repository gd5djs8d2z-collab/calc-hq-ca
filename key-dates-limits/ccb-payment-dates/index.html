When the CCB actually lands

The CRA aims for the 20th of each month. When the 20th falls on a weekend or a federal holiday the payment goes out earlier, never later — which is why June 19, September 18 and December 11 sit off the pattern in 2026. December is always paid well ahead of the holidays. If you're on direct deposit the money is usually in the account that morning; a mailed cheque can take another five to ten business days, which is the CRA's own wait-before-you-call window.

One quirk worth knowing: if your total annual CCB entitlement is small (under $240), the CRA pays it as a single lump sum in July instead of dribbling it out monthly — so a missing "monthly" payment isn't always a problem.

The July recalculation — why your amount changes

Every July the CRA recalculates the CCB using the adjusted family net income from the previous year's tax return. Payments from July 2026 through June 2027 are based on your 2025 income. Nothing about your children has to change for the amount to move — a raise in 2025 shows up as a smaller cheque starting July 2026, and a lower-income year works the other way.

This is also why filing matters more than usual: both you and your spouse or common-law partner have to file a return every year, even with no income, or the payments stop until you do. The same July reset applies to the GST/HST credit and the Ontario Trillium Benefit. To see what the new amount should be, run your numbers through the CCB calculator.

GST/HST credit, Trillium and the "Canada PRO" mystery deposit

The GST/HST credit is quarterly rather than monthly, and in 2026 it changed names: the CRA lists the January and April payments under the old name and the July and October ones as the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit (formerly the GST/HST credit). It's the same quarterly payment, so if you were expecting "GST" on your July statement and saw something else, that's why.

In Ontario, the Ontario Trillium Benefit bundles three credits — the Ontario energy and property tax credit, the Northern Ontario energy credit and the Ontario sales tax credit — into one monthly payment around the 10th. It lands as "Canada PRO" on bank statements, which is the single most-searched mystery deposit in Ontario. If your OTB entitlement is $360 or less you get it as one lump sum in July instead of monthly, and you can also elect a single June payment for the whole year.

Common questions

What are the CCB payment dates for 2026?

January 20, February 20, March 20, April 20, May 20, June 19, July 20, August 20, September 18, October 20, November 20 and December 11. When the 20th is a weekend or holiday the CRA pays earlier, never later.

Why did my CCB change in July?

The CRA recalculates every July from your prior year's tax return — July 2026 to June 2027 payments use your 2025 adjusted family net income. Both spouses must file every year or payments stop.

When is the GST/HST credit paid in 2026?

Quarterly: January 5 and April 2 as the GST/HST credit, then July 3 and October 5 as the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit (formerly the GST/HST credit) — same stream, new name.

What is a Canada PRO deposit?

It's the bank-statement label for CRA-paid provincial benefits — in Ontario, the Ontario Trillium Benefit (OEPTC + Northern Ontario energy credit + Ontario sales tax credit), normally paid around the 10th each month.
