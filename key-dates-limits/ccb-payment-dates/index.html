When the CCB actually lands

The CRA aims for the 20th of each month. When the 20th falls on a weekend or a federal holiday the payment goes out earlier, never later — which is why June 19, September 18 and December 11 sit off the pattern in 2026. December is always paid well ahead of the holidays. If you're on direct deposit the money is usually in the account that morning; a mailed cheque can take another five to ten business days, which is the CRA's own wait-before-you-call window.

One quirk worth knowing: if your total benefit for the year is less than $240, the CRA doesn't pay monthly at all — you get it as one lump sum with the July payment. So a missing "monthly" cheque isn't always a problem.

The July recalculation — why your amount changes

Every July the CRA recalculates the CCB using the adjusted family net income from the previous year's tax return. Payments from July 2026 through June 2027 are based on your 2025 income. Nothing about your children has to change for the amount to move — a raise in 2025 shows up as a smaller cheque starting July 2026, and a lower-income year works the other way.

This is also why filing matters more than usual: both you and your spouse or common-law partner have to file a return every year, even with no income, or the payments stop until you do. The same July reset applies to the GST/HST credit and the Ontario Trillium Benefit. To see what the new amount should be, run your numbers through the CCB calculator.

GST/HST credit, Trillium and the "Canada PRO" mystery deposit

The GST/HST credit is quarterly rather than monthly, and in 2026 it was renamed. The CRA is explicit about it: the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit (CGEB) replaced the GST/HST credit in July 2026, and under the new name "the eligibility, payment calculation, and structure is the same as it was for the GST/HST credit." That's why the January and April 2026 payments are listed under the old name and July and October under the new one. If you were expecting "GST" on your July statement and saw something else, that's the reason — it's the same quarterly payment stream. One substantive change did come with it: a 25% increase to the benefit amount starting July 2026, which the CRA says stays in place for five years (2026 to 2031).

In Ontario, the Ontario Trillium Benefit bundles three credits — the Ontario energy and property tax credit, the Northern Ontario energy credit and the Ontario sales tax credit — into one monthly payment around the 10th. Many banks display the deposit as "Canada PRO" rather than naming the credit, which is why it so often turns up as a mystery deposit — that label is a bank-side convention, not CRA wording, so don't expect to find it on a government page. Two rules shift the schedule: when the 10th falls on a weekend or statutory holiday the OTB is issued on the last working day before the 10th (which is why January, May and October sit early in 2026), and if your 2026 annual entitlement is $500 or less it's paid as one lump sum in the first payment month instead of monthly. You can also elect on your return to take the whole year in a single payment — in that case, if your entitlement is over $500, it's issued at the end of the benefit year on June 10, 2027.

Common questions

What are the CCB payment dates for 2026?

January 20, February 20, March 20, April 20, May 20, June 19, July 20, August 20, September 18, October 20, November 20 and December 11. When the 20th is a weekend or holiday the CRA pays earlier, never later.

Why did my CCB change in July?

The CRA recalculates every July from your prior year's tax return — July 2026 to June 2027 payments use your 2025 adjusted family net income. Both spouses must file every year or payments stop.

When is the GST/HST credit paid in 2026?

Quarterly: January 5 and April 2 as the GST/HST credit, then July 3 and October 5 as the Canada Groceries and Essentials Benefit (CGEB), which replaced it in July 2026. The CRA says eligibility, calculation and structure are unchanged — same stream, new name, plus a 25% increase to the amount from July 2026 through 2031.

What is a Canada PRO deposit?

It's how many banks display a CRA-paid provincial benefit — in Ontario, the Ontario Trillium Benefit (OEPTC + Northern Ontario energy credit + Ontario sales tax credit), paid on or just before the 10th each month. It's a bank-side label, not official CRA wording.
