Ontario Land Transfer Tax Calculator (2026)

Buying in Toronto means paying land transfer tax twice — once to the province, once to the city — and the first-time-buyer rebates are two different amounts. This works out both taxes and both rebates so you see one honest total.

Total land transfer tax payable

$0

Why Toronto buyers pay twice

Everywhere in Ontario you pay the provincial land transfer tax. Buy inside the City of Toronto and you also pay the city's Municipal Land Transfer Tax on top — a second bill, calculated separately, due the same day at closing. Up to $3 million the two use identical brackets, so the municipal tax essentially mirrors the provincial one and roughly doubles your total. That's the single biggest closing-cost surprise for Toronto buyers, and it's why a downtown condo carries far more tax than the same-priced house in Mississauga or Hamilton.

Both taxes are marginal. Each rate applies only to the portion of the price that falls inside its band — so the first $55,000 is always taxed at 0.5%, never your whole price at the top rate. The calculator adds up the bands for each tax and, in Toronto, sums the two.

The rates, band by band

The Ontario provincial rates:

  • 0.5% on the first $55,000
  • 1.0% from $55,000 to $250,000
  • 1.5% from $250,000 to $400,000
  • 2.0% from $400,000 to $2,000,000
  • 2.5% above $2,000,000 (homes with one or two units)

Toronto's municipal rates match the province exactly up to $2,000,000, add a 2.5% band from $2M to $3M, and then climb for luxury homes under rates the city set on April 1, 2026: 4.40% from $3M–$4M, 5.45% from $4M–$5M, 6.50% from $5M–$10M, 7.55% from $10M–$20M, and 8.60% above $20M. So above $3 million a Toronto purchase costs sharply more than the provincial tax alone.

Two rebates, two different amounts

First-time buyers get a rebate against each tax — and the amounts aren't the same. The provincial rebate is up to $4,000, which wipes out the Ontario LTT on a home up to about $368,000. The Toronto municipal rebate is up to $4,475, which clears the city LTT up to $400,000. They're claimed separately against their own tax, so a first-time buyer in Toronto collects both — as much as $8,475 off — while a first-time buyer anywhere else in Ontario gets the $4,000 provincial rebate only. Plenty of calculators apply a single rebate and quietly understate the benefit for Toronto buyers; this one applies each rebate to the tax it belongs to.

Common questions

Do you pay land transfer tax twice in Toronto?

Yes — the Ontario provincial LTT and the City of Toronto municipal LTT, both due at closing. Up to $3 million they share the same brackets, so the city tax roughly doubles the provincial one. Outside Toronto you pay the provincial tax only.

How much is land transfer tax in Ontario?

0.5% to $55,000, 1% to $250,000, 1.5% to $400,000, 2% to $2,000,000, and 2.5% above $2,000,000 on one- or two-unit homes. It's marginal — each rate hits only the slice of the price in that band. A $600,000 home is $8,475 provincially.

What is the first-time buyer rebate?

A refund of land transfer tax for qualifying first-time buyers: up to $4,000 provincially (full rebate to about $368,000) and, in Toronto, up to $4,475 municipally (full rebate to $400,000). A Toronto first-time buyer can claim both.

Are the Toronto and Ontario rebates the same amount?

No. The provincial rebate caps at $4,000, the Toronto rebate at $4,475, each against its own tax. A Toronto first-time buyer gets both — up to $8,475 combined — not a single figure.
